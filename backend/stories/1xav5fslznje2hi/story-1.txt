Our solar system has eight planets and five dwarf planets. Jupiter is the largest, 11 times wider than Earth. Saturn, known for its rings, is nine times wider. Uranus and Neptune are about four times wider. Earth is the fifth largest. Venus, Mars, and Mercury follow, with Mercury being the smallest. Dwarf planets include Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres, with Pluto being the largest.
